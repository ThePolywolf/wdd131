Overview

Purpose

The purpose of this site is to teach the viewer about the incredible air superiority of the United States military, backed up by impressive statistics and facts.

Audience

The audience for this site is someone who does not understand much about the military, or how strong the US military is.

Dynamic elements

This site has two main dynamic elements: A submission form for a newsletter (changes to a "received" message after fake submission), a mini quiz about the content of the site (choose random 6/10 questions with results afterwards with retake).

Content

Home page

Welcome to American Air Superiority

Explore the dynamic world of the United States' aerial forces, a cornerstone of modern defense and security. From cutting-edge aircraft to the strategic missions that secure our skies, we showcase the unparalleled might and ingenuity of American aviation.

Our Mission

We aim to educate and inspire by highlighting the role of air superiority in national defense. By connecting people to the facts, achievements, and ongoing advancements of U.S. aerial forces, we celebrate the power and precision behind America’s wings.

What You’ll Discover

  • Insights into the Air Force, Navy, Marine Corps, Army, and Coast Guard aviation.
  • Unveiling aircraft technologies, missions, and milestones.
  • A comprehensive view of air combat strategy and the people who make it possible.

Dive into the stories that define American air dominance and explore the forces shaping the future of aerial warfare.

Stats

U.S. Air Force Aviation Stats: Number of active-duty personnel. Total aircraft in operation (fighter jets, bombers, drones). Annual budget allocation for air superiority. Mission success rates or hours flown annually.

U.S. Navy Aviation Stats: Total aircraft carriers equipped with air units. Types and counts of naval aircraft (fighter jets, helicopters). Number of naval aviators. Missions supporting naval operations and maritime security.

U.S. Marine Corps Aviation Stats: Focus on vertical take-off and attack aircraft. Number of squadrons. Integration of aviation with ground forces.

U.S. Army Aviation Stats: Total helicopters (attack, transport, reconnaissance). UAV deployment and capabilities. Support operations for ground troops.

U.S. Coast Guard Aviation Stats: Number of rescue and patrol aircraft. Annual search-and-rescue missions completed. Areas of operation (domestic waterways, international waters).

Quick Facts Section: Total U.S. aerial assets in operation. Combined military budget for aerial forces. Notable aircraft models across all branches. Training hours required for a new pilot in each branch.
